# Emotion Taxonomy for Content Creation

## Primary Emotions

### Curiosity
**Description**: A desire to learn, explore, or understand something unknown. Drives readers to continue reading to resolve the open loop.
**Engagement Effect**: High click-through and read-completion. Works best as an opening hook.
**Trigger Phrases**: "What most people don't know…", "The real reason…", "Here's what changed my mind…"

### Urgency
**Description**: A sense that time is limited or action is required now. Creates momentum and reduces procrastination.
**Engagement Effect**: High conversion and call-to-action response. Powerful for deadline-driven content.
**Trigger Phrases**: "Before it's too late…", "This window is closing…", "Act now or miss…"

### Awe
**Description**: Wonder and admiration triggered by something vast, impressive, or paradigm-shifting. Makes the reader feel small in a positive way.
**Engagement Effect**: High share rate. People share awe-inspiring content to signal their own taste and intelligence.
**Trigger Phrases**: "This completely changed how I see…", "The scale of this is hard to grasp…", "I had no idea that…"

### Frustration
**Description**: Tension from a problem that resonates. The reader feels understood and validated. Pain-point acknowledgment.
**Engagement Effect**: High comment rate. Readers share their own frustrations, creating conversation.
**Trigger Phrases**: "Every time I see this, I cringe…", "Why does no one talk about…", "The broken system no one admits…"

### Fear
**Description**: Concern about a potential negative outcome. Activates protective instincts and attention.
**Engagement Effect**: High open rates and initial attention. Must be followed by hope or a solution to avoid reader abandonment.
**Trigger Phrases**: "The silent career killer…", "What you're risking by ignoring…", "Most professionals overlook this until it's too late…"

### Hope
**Description**: Optimism about a better future state. Inspires action and creates positive association with the author.
**Engagement Effect**: High save rate. People bookmark hopeful content to revisit when they need motivation.
**Trigger Phrases**: "It doesn't have to be this way…", "Here's what's possible…", "I've seen this work for people just like you…"

### Pride
**Description**: Satisfaction from achievement, identity affirmation, or belonging to an aspirational group.
**Engagement Effect**: High like and share rate. People share content that reflects well on their identity.
**Trigger Phrases**: "If you do this, you're already ahead of 95%…", "This is what separates good from great…", "You've earned this…"

### Surprise
**Description**: Violation of expectation in a positive or dramatic way. The brain pays extra attention to the unexpected.
**Engagement Effect**: High virality. Counterintuitive findings spread because they signal novelty.
**Trigger Phrases**: "Counterintuitively…", "Against everything I was taught…", "The data showed the opposite of what I expected…"

### Empathy
**Description**: Recognition of shared human experience. The reader feels seen, understood, and less alone.
**Engagement Effect**: High comment rate with personal disclosures. Builds deep audience loyalty.
**Trigger Phrases**: "If you've ever felt like…", "I know this feeling well…", "You're not the only one who…"

### FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)
**Description**: Anxiety about being left behind while others advance. Combines fear and social comparison.
**Engagement Effect**: High urgency-driven action. Works especially well for trend, opportunity, and skill content.
**Trigger Phrases**: "While you're waiting, others are already…", "The window for this is closing…", "The early movers will own this…"

## Emotion Blends

### Bittersweet
**Components**: Nostalgia + Hope
**Description**: A wistful appreciation for what was combined with optimism for what can be. Common in reflection posts, career transitions, and milestone celebrations.
**Best For**: Personal journey content, year-in-review, industry retrospectives.

### Righteous Anger
**Components**: Frustration + Pride + Hope
**Description**: Moral indignation at an injustice or broken system, combined with conviction that better is possible. Energizing rather than despairing.
**Best For**: Industry critique, advocacy, contrarian takes on established norms.

### Nostalgic Aspiration
**Components**: Nostalgia + Awe + Hope
**Description**: Honoring lessons from the past while pointing toward a grander future. Creates continuity between tradition and innovation.
**Best For**: Leadership content, brand storytelling, culture posts.

### Competitive Curiosity
**Components**: Curiosity + FOMO + Pride
**Description**: The drive to learn and grow fueled by awareness that others are advancing. Motivating without being threatening.
**Best For**: Skill-building content, tool roundups, professional development posts.

## Intensity Scale

| Level | Label | Description | Usage Guidance |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------------|
| 1-2 | Gentle | Soft emotional resonance; warm, reflective tone | Thought leadership, quiet wisdom posts |
| 3-4 | Moderate | Clear emotional signal; reader notices the feeling | Most everyday content; builds connection |
| 5-6 | Engaged | Strong emotional pull; reader feels invested | Top-of-funnel hooks, stories with stakes |
| 7-8 | Intense | High arousal; reader may share or comment immediately | Viral bids, controversy, urgent calls to action |
| 9-10 | Peak | Maximum emotional force; risks feeling manipulative if not earned | Crisis communication, milestone announcements, rare use |

**Guidance**: Most LinkedIn content performs best at 4-6. Intensities above 7 should be reserved for content where the emotional claim is fully justified by substance. Intensity mismatches (high emotion, thin substance) erode trust.

## Channel-Emotion Fit

### LinkedIn
**Best Emotions**: Curiosity, Pride, Awe, Empathy, Hope
**Avoid**: Raw Fear, excessive FOMO (feels manipulative in a professional context)
**Notes**: Professional audiences respond to emotions that enhance their identity or knowledge. Vulnerability works when paired with a lesson.

### Instagram
**Best Emotions**: Awe, Pride, Surprise, Hope, Nostalgia (Bittersweet)
**Avoid**: Frustration as primary (too negative for visual-first scrolling)
**Notes**: Visual-first platform; emotion must be readable in the first 1-2 seconds. High-intensity awe and aspiration perform best.

### Email
**Best Emotions**: Curiosity, Urgency, Fear (with resolution), Hope
**Avoid**: Pure awe (hard to land without visuals); FOMO as a closer
**Notes**: Subject line must land the primary emotion immediately. Fear works when the email body delivers the solution.

### WhatsApp
**Best Emotions**: Empathy, Surprise, Urgency, FOMO
**Avoid**: Long-arc emotions like Nostalgia or complex Bittersweet blends
**Notes**: Short-form, conversational. Emotions should be immediate and shareable. Works best with Surprise or Urgency to drive forwarding.

### Telegram
**Best Emotions**: Curiosity, Urgency, Righteous Anger, FOMO
**Avoid**: Gentle intensities (1-3); they get ignored in high-volume channels
**Notes**: Community and news-driven platform. Competitive Curiosity and Righteous Anger drive engagement in professional Telegram groups.
