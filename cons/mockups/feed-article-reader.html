OpenAI rolls out GPT-4o upgrades with real-time voice and vision capabilities

TechCrunch By Sarah Perez 2 hours ago 4 min read

OpenAI today announced upgrades to GPT-4o that bring full real-time voice and vision support to its flagship model. Voice latency drops to roughly 320 ms — matching human conversational pacing — and vision now handles streaming video for live tutoring, accessibility, and field-service use cases.

In a brief livestream, the company's research lead Mira Murati demoed a back-and-forth conversation with GPT-4o that handled interruptions, emotional cues, and multilingual switching without a perceptible pause. Independent benchmarks shared with TechCrunch show a 40% latency drop versus the previous voice-mode preview, with notably better handling of background noise and overlapping speech.

The enterprise tier is the more consequential announcement. API access ships next week, and OpenAI is replacing the older token-based audio billing with per-minute streaming pricing. That shifts cost predictability significantly for high-volume use cases like contact centers, where audio can pile up unpredictably under load.

“If voice models close the latency gap, typing becomes a niche input — the way handwriting did when keyboards arrived.”

— excerpted from the company's developer blog post

Notably, video-in is restricted to 480p at launch and capped at 30-minute sessions per call — limits the company says are due to GPU capacity rather than capability. Higher-tier customers can apply for an exemption that lifts the cap to 4K and 2-hour sessions.

The move puts pressure on Anthropic and Google, both of which have hinted at multimodal roadmaps for Q3 but have yet to ship comparable real-time experiences. Anthropic in particular has been positioning around enterprise compliance posture rather than raw capability — a strategy that may need to adjust if voice latency becomes the new benchmark customers care about.

For developers already on the Assistants API, the upgrade should require no code changes. OpenAI's documentation lists a single new parameter — voice_streaming — that enables the lower-latency mode. Pricing details and rate limits are expected to land alongside the GA rollout next Tuesday.
