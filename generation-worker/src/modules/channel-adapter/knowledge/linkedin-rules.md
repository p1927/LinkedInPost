# LinkedIn Formatting Rules

## Character Limits
- **Post body**: 3,000 characters maximum
- **"See more" fold**: ~210 characters — the first ~3 lines are visible before the reader must click "see more"
- **Hashtag recommendation**: 3–5 hashtags per post (more suppresses reach)
- **Comment length**: 1,250 characters maximum

## "See more" Behavior
- Everything after approximately 210 characters (or 3 short lines) is hidden behind a "see more" click
- The hook (first 1–2 sentences) must stand alone and compel the click
- Cliffhangers, open loops, and questions placed before the fold maximize click-through
- Do NOT front-load hashtags or bullet lists before the fold — lead with the narrative hook

## Formatting Rules
- **Line breaks**: Single blank line between paragraphs is standard; double blank lines create visible whitespace sections
- **Bold / Italic**: Not natively supported in post text — use Unicode bold/italic sparingly or not at all
- **Emojis**: Accepted and effective as bullet replacements or attention anchors; use ≤1 per paragraph
- **Lists**: Use plain dashes or bullets (–, •) or numbered lists; markdown syntax not rendered
- **Hashtags**: Append at the bottom of the post, not inline mid-sentence; lowercase preferred

## Hashtag Strategy
- 3–5 targeted hashtags outperform 10+ hashtags
- Mix niche hashtags (under 100K followers) with broad ones (under 1M)
- Avoid hashtags with millions of followers — content drowns immediately
- Place all hashtags in the last 2 lines of the post body

## Algorithm Notes
- External links in body copy reduce distribution — move URLs to first comment
- Native documents (PDF carousels) receive strong algorithmic boost
- Text-only posts often outperform link posts on reach
