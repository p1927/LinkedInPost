# Engineering Manager

id: engineering-manager
name: Engineering Manager

---

## Concerns

- Keeping the team productive and unblocked while managing stakeholder expectations and shifting priorities
- Retaining top engineers in a competitive hiring market where their direct reports get recruited constantly
- Technical debt accumulating faster than the team can address it without slowing feature delivery
- Navigating the tension between moving fast and maintaining code quality, security, and system reliability
- Growing as a leader without losing touch with the technical depth that earned them credibility
- Demonstrating the value of engineering investments to non-technical executives and product stakeholders

## Ambitions

- Building a high-performing, psychologically safe team that delivers consistently without burning out
- Earning a path to director or VP of Engineering through demonstrated organizational impact
- Creating engineering culture and processes that scale as the team and codebase grow
- Being known as a manager people want to work for — the kind who accelerates careers, not just ships
- Shipping meaningful technical improvements (architecture, reliability, developer experience) alongside product work
- Developing a personal leadership philosophy they can articulate and teach

## Current Focus

Balancing delivery against technical health — keeping the team shipping roadmap items while carving out capacity for foundational improvements that prevent future bottlenecks, all while supporting individual growth plans for direct reports.

## Habits

- Runs weekly 1:1s and team syncs with structured agendas; takes notes and follows up on action items
- Reviews pull requests to stay technically grounded, even when no longer the primary contributor
- Monitors team velocity and cycle time to identify friction points before they become crises
- Reads engineering leadership content — newsletters, books, Hacker News, Lenny's, StaffEng
- Spends time in cross-functional meetings (product, design, data) to understand upstream context
- Writes documentation — RFC templates, decision logs, onboarding guides — to reduce bus factor

## Language

Precise and context-aware. Comfortable with both engineering terminology (latency, observability, CI/CD, incident postmortem) and management language (feedback loops, growth plans, RACI, roadmap tradeoffs). Prefers directness but values emotional intelligence in communication. Skeptical of management frameworks that feel like buzzword rebranding. Responds to concrete examples over abstract principles.

## Decision Drivers

- Team impact — would this help the team move faster, grow, or suffer less friction?
- Long-term vs. short-term tradeoff clarity — what are we trading away, and is it worth it?
- Trust in the source — has this person managed engineers at scale, or are they theorizing?
- Process fit — does this add overhead, or does it replace overhead with something lighter?
- Fairness and consistency — decisions must be explainable and applied uniformly across the team
- Reversibility — prefers decisions that can be undone if they produce the wrong outcome

## Pain Points

- Caught between engineering needs and product demands, rarely feeling like they can fully satisfy either
- One-on-ones reveal problems that should have surfaced earlier, making coaching feel reactive not proactive
- Technical conversations with non-engineers often require re-explaining the same fundamentals repeatedly
- Hiring pipelines are slow, unpredictable, and consume disproportionate managerial energy
- Meeting load creeps up to the point where strategic thinking and real work get squeezed out
- Feedback from above is often too vague to act on and too infrequent to course-correct in time
