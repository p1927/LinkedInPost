# Hook Types

A hook is the first 1–2 lines of a post. It is the single highest-leverage element in LinkedIn content — it determines whether the "see more" threshold is crossed.

---

## Question Hook

**Description**: Opens with a direct question that the reader cannot easily dismiss. Works by activating the reader's own uncertainty or curiosity.

**Example**: "What would you do if your best engineer quit tomorrow with no warning?"

**Best for**: Thought-provoking topics, audience self-reflection, posts targeting a specific pain or decision. Avoid generic yes/no questions — specificity is the difference between a scroll-stopper and noise.

---

## Stat Hook

**Description**: Opens with a surprising, specific number that reframes what the reader thought they knew. The shock or dissonance keeps them reading to find out what it means.

**Example**: "73% of first-round interviews are rejected in the first 90 seconds — before a word is spoken."

**Best for**: Data-driven content, authority-building posts, B2B audiences, any claim that benefits from empirical grounding. Numbers must be specific and traceable — vague percentages backfire with skeptical audiences.

---

## Contrarian Hook

**Description**: Opens by directly contradicting a widely held belief or conventional wisdom. Creates cognitive dissonance that demands resolution.

**Example**: "Hustle culture is not making you more productive. It is making you more mediocre."

**Best for**: Thought leadership, opinion pieces, expertise-signaling posts. High-risk, high-reward — will generate strong agreement and strong disagreement. Best when the contrarian claim can be fully defended inside the post.

---

## Story-Open Hook

**Description**: Opens in the middle of a scene or moment, dropping the reader directly into a specific situation. Creates narrative pull through unresolved tension.

**Example**: "It was 11:47 PM. The demo was in nine hours. And nothing worked."

**Best for**: Personal brand content, transformation stories, case studies, entrepreneurship narratives. Requires a payoff — the story must connect meaningfully to the post's core insight.

---

## Challenge Hook

**Description**: Opens with a direct dare or challenge to the reader. Activates identity and competitive instinct.

**Example**: "Name one decision you made this year that made your team genuinely better. Not busier. Better."

**Best for**: Leadership content, high-agency audiences, posts targeting senior professionals. Must feel like a genuine invitation to reflect, not a gotcha or trick question.

---

## List Hook

**Description**: Opens by signaling a structured list of items, creating an implicit promise of organized value and a clear end point. Reduces perceived effort required to finish the post.

**Example**: "5 things I wish someone told me before my first VP role:"

**Best for**: Educational content, how-to posts, tactical guides, posts with dense information. Works with audiences who value clarity and efficiency over narrative depth.

---

## Confession Hook

**Description**: Opens with a candid admission of a mistake, failure, embarrassing belief, or previously held wrong view. Disarms skepticism through radical honesty and signals growth.

**Example**: "I spent two years optimizing a metric that didn't matter. Here is how I finally found out."

**Best for**: Personal brand posts, founder content, mentorship-style content, building parasocial trust. The confession must be genuine — manufactured vulnerability reads as manipulative.

---

## Hook Selection Rules

### By Audience Type

| Audience | Best Hook Types |
|----------|-----------------|
| Executives / senior leaders | Challenge, Contrarian, Stat |
| Founders / entrepreneurs | Confession, Story-Open, Contrarian |
| Individual contributors | Question, List, Stat |
| Mixed / broad professional | List, Question, Stat |

### By Content Goal

| Goal | Best Hook Types |
|------|-----------------|
| Drive comments | Question, Challenge, Contrarian |
| Drive shares | Stat, Contrarian, List |
| Build authority | Stat, Contrarian, 4Ps-style |
| Build connection | Confession, Story-Open |
| Drive follows | Confession, Story-Open, Challenge |

### By Post Length

- Short posts (< 400 chars): Question, Stat, Contrarian — direct, no preamble needed
- Medium posts (400–900 chars): List, Challenge, Confession — promise structure or depth
- Long posts (900+ chars): Story-Open, Confession, Monroe-style — earn the long read
