# Viral Post Structures

Viral posts follow recognizable structural patterns that trigger engagement. Each pattern exploits a specific psychological driver.

## 1. Contrarian Take

**Structure:** Assert the opposite of conventional wisdom → support with evidence or experience → reframe the accepted view.

**Example shape:**
> "Everyone says X. They're wrong. Here's what's actually happening…"

**Engagement Driver:** Intellectual friction. Disagreement compels people to read further (to confirm or challenge the view) and comment to defend their position.

---

## 2. Relatable Struggle

**Structure:** Describe a universal frustration or failure in hyper-specific detail → validate the reader's experience → offer a reframe or small resolution.

**Example shape:**
> "I spent 6 months doing X the hard way before I realized…"

**Engagement Driver:** Recognition + relief. The reader feels seen and saves/shares so others know they are not alone.

---

## 3. Surprising Data

**Structure:** Lead with a counterintuitive statistic → explain the implication → connect to actionable insight.

**Example shape:**
> "Only 3% of [group] does X — yet they account for 40% of [outcome]."

**Engagement Driver:** Pattern disruption. Data that violates expectations forces a cognitive stop and invites curiosity about the mechanism.

---

## 4. Humble Brag Story

**Structure:** Acknowledge a mistake or failure → reveal a turning-point moment → share the result modestly with a lesson.

**Example shape:**
> "I failed at X for two years. Then one conversation changed everything."

**Engagement Driver:** Authenticity + aspiration. Vulnerability earns trust; the success component delivers reward and inspires.

---

## 5. Hot Take

**Structure:** Deliver a sharp, polarizing opinion in the first sentence → support it briefly → invite counter-responses.

**Example shape:**
> "Unpopular opinion: [X] is the laziest advice in [industry]."

**Engagement Driver:** Identity signaling. Readers who agree share to signal affiliation; readers who disagree comment to defend their views.

---

## 6. Listicle

**Structure:** Numbered or bulleted list of tips, mistakes, or lessons → each item is self-contained and scannable → close with a unifying insight.

**Example shape:**
> "7 things I wish someone told me before [X]:"

**Engagement Driver:** Completion bias + utility. Readers scan the list and save it for future reference; numbered format triggers the need to read every item.

---

## 7. Before / After

**Structure:** Paint the "before" state in vivid, painful detail → describe the single change or insight → reveal the transformed "after" state.

**Example shape:**
> "12 months ago: [struggling state]. Today: [transformed state]. The difference was one decision."

**Engagement Driver:** Transformation desire. Readers project themselves into the before-state and are pulled forward by the implied possibility.
