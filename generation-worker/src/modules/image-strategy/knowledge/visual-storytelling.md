# Visual Storytelling

## Image Types by Narrative Style

### Transformational
Shows contrast between a before-state and an after-state. The visual tension between the two creates narrative momentum.
- **Examples**: Side-by-side comparisons, split-frame images, progress timelines
- **When to Use**: Milestone content, case studies, growth narratives

### Aspirational
Projects an idealized future or peak outcome. The viewer sees themselves achieving the state depicted.
- **Examples**: Clean workspace, summit view, team celebration, product launch moment
- **When to Use**: Motivational content, vision statements, career achievement posts

### Documentary
Captures an authentic, unposed moment. Signals honesty and insider access.
- **Examples**: Candid meeting photos, behind-the-scenes shots, process documentation
- **When to Use**: Thought leadership, culture posts, transparency narratives

### Conceptual / Abstract
Uses metaphor or symbolism to represent an idea rather than depicting it literally.
- **Examples**: Chess pieces, architecture, natural patterns, geometric shapes
- **When to Use**: Complex ideas, abstract frameworks, philosophical or strategic content

### Data-Driven
Presents quantitative information as visual hierarchy. Numbers and charts anchor credibility.
- **Examples**: Infographics, annotated charts, comparative tables, stat callouts
- **When to Use**: Research shares, performance results, argument-with-evidence posts

### Human-Centered
Faces and human presence create immediate emotional connection and identity.
- **Examples**: Portraits, team photos, audience shots, community gatherings
- **When to Use**: Personal brand content, empathy-driven narratives, community recognition

## Image Types by Channel

### LinkedIn Feed
- **Best Performing**: Professional documentary, aspirational, human-centered
- **Optimal Ratio**: 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait) — takes more vertical real estate
- **Text Overlay**: Minimal — let the image breathe; headline on image if strong visual hook
- **Avoid**: Stock photo clichés (handshakes, generic suits), low-resolution lifestyle shots

### LinkedIn Article / Newsletter
- **Best Performing**: Conceptual, data-driven, transformational
- **Optimal Ratio**: 16:9 (header) or 1:1 (inline)
- **Text Overlay**: Headers and section markers acceptable
- **Avoid**: Purely decorative images that add no informational value

### LinkedIn Story / Carousel
- **Best Performing**: Sequential transformational or data-driven
- **Optimal Ratio**: 9:16 (portrait-first for carousel covers)
- **Text Overlay**: Each slide should carry a complete micro-message
- **Avoid**: Dense or cluttered compositions that don't read at glance
