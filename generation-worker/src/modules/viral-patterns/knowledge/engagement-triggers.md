# Engagement Triggers

Triggers are design elements that activate a specific engagement action. Each trigger is classified by the primary response it targets.

---

## Comment Triggers

Comment triggers provoke a reader to voice an opinion, share an experience, or ask a question.

- **Open question at end**: Close with a direct question that has no single right answer ("What's your experience with X?")
- **Polarizing statement**: Take a clear side on a contested topic so readers feel compelled to agree loudly or push back
- **Incomplete story**: Hint at a result without fully revealing it, inviting readers to guess or demand a follow-up
- **Call for opinions**: Explicitly solicit the reader's take ("Drop your answer below" / "I'm curious what you think")
- **Shared frustration prompt**: Name a pain and ask if others have experienced it ("Anyone else deal with this?")
- **Contrarian challenge**: Invite dissent by framing the post as a hypothesis ("Prove me wrong")
- **Identity challenge**: Frame a belief as something only a certain kind of person would hold, triggering alignment or protest

---

## Share Triggers

Share triggers motivate a reader to distribute the post to their network or tag someone specific.

- **Universal lesson from niche experience**: Turn a specific struggle into a widely applicable insight — readers tag people who "need to see this"
- **Compact utility**: Deliver a checklist, framework, or decision tool that will remain useful weeks later
- **Surprising fact or reframe**: Provide information that changes how someone sees a familiar topic — novelty drives forward-sharing
- **Social currency**: Make the sharer look smart, empathetic, or well-informed by association with the post
- **"Tag someone who…" prompt**: Explicit tag request with a specific persona descriptor ("Tag a founder who needs this")
- **Timely or trending angle**: Connect the content to a current event or seasonal moment that gives urgency to sharing
- **Emotional validation**: Articulate a feeling so precisely that readers share it as a form of self-expression

---

## Save Triggers

Save triggers encourage a reader to bookmark the post for future reference.

- **Reference list**: Numbered list of tools, resources, or tips that cannot be fully absorbed in one reading
- **Step-by-step process**: A sequential guide readers will want to revisit while implementing
- **Dense information density**: Pack enough insight into a short post that a single read feels insufficient
- **Template or fill-in-the-blank structure**: Provide a reusable format readers will want to apply later
- **"Read this before you…" framing**: Position the post as essential preparation for a near-future action
- **Evergreen truth**: A principle or observation that remains relevant regardless of when it is read — worth keeping
- **Cheat-sheet format**: Compressed reference (definitions, comparisons, decision criteria) readers will return to
