# CTA Patterns

A call to action (CTA) is the final instruction given to the reader. It must match the post's intent, the platform's affordances, and the audience's likely willingness to act. The wrong CTA undermines an otherwise strong post.

---

## Intent: Engage

**Description**: Invites comments, opinions, replies, or reactions. Maximizes dwell time and algorithmic distribution through conversation signals.

**Examples**:
- "What would you add to this list?"
- "Have you seen this pattern in your own team? Drop it in the comments."
- "Agree or disagree — I genuinely want to know."
- "What's the version of this you're dealing with right now?"
- "Reply with the one word that describes your experience with this."

**Best for**: Thought leadership posts, opinion pieces, any post targeting algorithmic amplification on LinkedIn. Engagement CTAs work best when the question is specific and answerable in 1–3 sentences.

---

## Intent: Click

**Description**: Directs the reader to take an external action — visit a link, read an article, download a resource, sign up for something.

**Examples**:
- "The full breakdown is in the link below."
- "If you want the template, it's linked in the first comment."
- "More detail on how we ran this in the article I've shared."
- "Three-minute read. Worth it. Link in bio."
- "Full research paper linked — it's dense but the abstract alone is worth it."

**Best for**: Content distribution, lead generation, product announcements, event promotion. Note: LinkedIn's algorithm deprioritizes posts with links in the main body — use first-comment link strategy where possible.

---

## Intent: Share

**Description**: Explicitly invites the reader to forward or repost the content to their network.

**Examples**:
- "If this landed for you, share it with someone who needs to hear it."
- "Tag a founder who would appreciate this."
- "Worth sharing with your team before your next planning cycle."
- "Forward this to the person on your team who handles this."
- "Share this with someone who's been in this situation."

**Best for**: High-value educational content, urgent or timely posts, posts with broad applicability. Share CTAs work best when the reader can immediately picture the specific person they would share it with.

---

## Intent: Follow

**Description**: Invites the reader to follow the author's profile for future content.

**Examples**:
- "Follow me for more frameworks like this."
- "I write about this every week — follow if you want to stay ahead of it."
- "If this was useful, you'll find the same in everything I post. Follow along."
- "I share one insight like this every Tuesday. Worth a follow if that's useful."

**Best for**: Personal brand building posts, new audience acquisition, content that performs well outside the author's existing followers. Use sparingly — overuse makes an author feel promotional rather than value-driven.

---

## Intent: None

**Description**: The post ends without a directive CTA. The content itself is complete and self-contained. No ask is made.

**Examples**:
- "That's it. Just wanted to say it plainly."
- Post ends on a resonant line that closes the narrative loop with no prompt to act.
- "Make of that what you will."
- Ends on a summary or restatement of the core insight.

**Best for**: Confessional posts, rants, short provocative takes, philosophical observations, high-conviction one-liners. A deliberately CTA-free post signals confidence and can paradoxically generate more organic engagement because it feels less transactional.

---

## CTA Selection Rules

### By Post Type

| Post Type | Best CTA Intent |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Opinion / hot take | Engage or None |
| Educational / how-to | Share or Engage |
| Personal story | None or Follow |
| Product / announcement | Click |
| Data / research | Share or Engage |
| Career / leadership | Engage or Follow |
| Rant / frustration | None or Engage |

### By Audience Size Context

- **Building audience (< 5k followers)**: Follow CTAs are more valuable — prioritize follow over engage
- **Mid-range (5k–50k)**: Engage CTAs for reach, Click for conversions
- **Large audience (50k+)**: Share and Engage for distribution, None for credibility

### By Funnel Stage

- **Top of funnel (awareness)**: Share, Engage
- **Middle of funnel (consideration)**: Click, Engage
- **Bottom of funnel (conversion)**: Click, direct and specific
- **Retention / community**: Engage, Follow

### One CTA Rule

Each post should have at most one explicit CTA. Multiple CTAs compete with each other and reduce the likelihood that any single one is acted upon. If a post has both a link and a question, choose one as primary.
