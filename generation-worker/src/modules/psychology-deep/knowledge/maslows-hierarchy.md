# Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs — Content Strategy Framework

## Overview

Maslow's hierarchy describes five levels of human motivation arranged in order of priority. Content that speaks to an active need level creates immediate resonance. The key insight for content strategy: people engage most deeply with content that addresses the level they are currently trying to move through — not a level they have already satisfied, and not one that feels unreachably aspirational.

---

## Level 1 — Physiological Needs

**Core drive:** Survival, sustenance, basic function.

**In a professional context:** Income stability, ability to keep the lights on, fear of job loss, financial runway.

### Content Targeting Strategy
- Lead with urgency and scarcity signals ("before you lose the opportunity", "what happens when X disappears")
- Frame solutions as essential, not optional
- Use concrete numbers, timelines, and direct outcomes
- Avoid abstract language; anchor to survival stakes

### Trigger Phrases
- "How to protect your income when..."
- "The one thing you can't afford to ignore"
- "What to do before your job is automated"
- "The financial floor every professional needs"

### Persona Fit
Best for: Career-changers under pressure, recent layoffs, early-career professionals with high financial anxiety, founders pre-revenue.

### Selection Rule
Choose Level 1 when the topic directly addresses job security, income threats, financial survival, or baseline resource scarcity.

---

## Level 2 — Safety Needs

**Core drive:** Stability, predictability, protection from threat.

**In a professional context:** Job security, reliable processes, risk mitigation, professional reputation protection, legal/regulatory compliance.

### Content Targeting Strategy
- Frame content as risk-reduction and protection
- Highlight what can go wrong without action (but not catastrophizing)
- Use reassurance tone: calm authority, step-by-step guidance
- Proof via established institutions, track records, certifications

### Trigger Phrases
- "How to protect yourself from..."
- "The safety net most professionals miss"
- "Why [X] is a risk you're probably underestimating"
- "How to build a career that can weather any storm"

### Persona Fit
Best for: Mid-career professionals during industry disruption, compliance-focused roles, professionals in regulated industries, anyone facing significant uncertainty.

### Selection Rule
Choose Level 2 when the topic is about reducing risk, building stability, navigating uncertainty, or protecting what already exists.

---

## Level 3 — Belonging Needs

**Core drive:** Connection, community, acceptance, love.

**In a professional context:** Team cohesion, peer recognition, industry community membership, not being left out, shared identity ("we" language).

### Content Targeting Strategy
- Use inclusive "we" framing and community language
- Highlight shared experiences and common struggles
- Reference tribes, communities, and in-group markers
- Build FOMO around missing out on community moments

### Trigger Phrases
- "Every [X professional] knows this feeling"
- "The conversation the industry is having right now"
- "What [community/group] figured out together"
- "You're not alone in dealing with..."

### Persona Fit
Best for: Remote workers feeling isolated, professionals new to an industry, people building teams, anyone targeting community-oriented audiences (e.g., DEI, culture-focused roles).

### Selection Rule
Choose Level 3 when the topic centers on community, shared identity, team dynamics, belonging, industry tribe, or the "you're not alone" frame.

---

## Level 4 — Esteem Needs

**Core drive:** Recognition, achievement, status, competence, respect.

**In a professional context:** Career advancement, being seen as expert, public recognition, promotions, impressive credentials, building a personal brand.

### Content Targeting Strategy
- Acknowledge the reader's competence and elevate their status
- Frame content as giving them an edge or insider advantage
- Use achievement-oriented language ("leaders who...", "top performers...")
- Reference status markers relevant to the audience (titles, companies, outcomes)

### Trigger Phrases
- "What separates good from great [X]"
- "How the best [professionals] approach this"
- "The moves that get you noticed as an expert"
- "Earn the reputation you deserve"

### Persona Fit
Best for: Ambitious mid-to-senior professionals, anyone building a personal brand, high performers seeking visibility, professionals in competitive fields.

### Selection Rule
Choose Level 4 when the topic is about career advancement, expertise signaling, recognition, reputation-building, or status in a field.

---

## Level 5 — Self-Actualization

**Core drive:** Meaning, purpose, full potential, growth, contribution.

**In a professional context:** Doing work that matters, legacy, mastery for its own sake, contribution to something larger, creative expression, full alignment between values and work.

### Content Targeting Strategy
- Speak to meaning and purpose, not just outcomes
- Frame the reader as a protagonist in a larger story
- Use reflective, philosophical, or visionary tone
- Invite depth: challenge assumptions, open new perspectives

### Trigger Phrases
- "What does it mean to truly succeed at..."
- "The work that will still matter in 10 years"
- "When you stop optimizing for the metrics and start optimizing for..."
- "The version of [X] you actually want to become"

### Persona Fit
Best for: Senior leaders, entrepreneurs with established businesses, creators, anyone at a career inflection point asking "what's next and why does it matter."

### Selection Rule
Choose Level 5 when the topic is existential, purpose-driven, legacy-focused, or about mastery and meaning rather than external achievement.

---

## Selection Rules Summary

| If the topic is about... | Use Level |
|--------------------------|-----------|
| Income threats, job loss, financial survival | 1 — Physiological |
| Risk, uncertainty, stability, protection | 2 — Safety |
| Community, belonging, shared identity | 3 — Belonging |
| Career advancement, expertise, recognition | 4 — Esteem |
| Meaning, purpose, legacy, mastery | 5 — Self-Actualization |

**Stacking Rule:** When a topic spans multiple levels, anchor the hook at the lowest active level (highest urgency) and resolve toward a higher level. Example: start with Safety anxiety, resolve with Esteem payoff.
