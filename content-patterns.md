Programmatic Patterns & Templates for LinkedIn, Instagram & Email

_Research-backed patterns that consistently drive reach, engagement & conversions_

# **Introduction**

Viral content is not luck - it is a repeatable system. Across LinkedIn, Instagram, and email, certain structural patterns consistently outperform everything else. This playbook documents those patterns with ready-to-use templates so you can produce high-performing content programmatically, at scale, without starting from scratch.

Each section covers the platform's core algorithm signals, the top viral patterns backed by 2025-2026 research, and a fill-in template for each pattern.

# **SECTION 1: LinkedIn**

**How the Algorithm Works**

LinkedIn's algorithm prioritises content that generates rapid, meaningful comments within the first 60-90 minutes of posting. Dwell time (how long users pause on your post) and saves are secondary signals. Carousels maximise dwell time; text posts with strong hooks drive comment velocity. In 2026, carousel posts, short bold text posts, and behind-the-scenes 'build-in-public' content dominate the feed.

| **Metric**       | **What to optimise for**                   |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Comment velocity | Get 10+ comments in the first 2 hours      |
| Dwell time       | Use carousels & long-form listicles        |
| Saves            | Reference posts, frameworks, and toolkits  |
| Shares           | Opinion posts, hot takes, and data reveals |
| Best post times  | Tue-Thu, 8-11 AM local time                |

## **Pattern 1 - The Story Arc (Personal Narrative)**

The single most viral LinkedIn format. A short personal story with a clear beginning, crisis, and lesson. Visual storytelling combined with authentic vulnerability generates up to 596% more engagement than plain text posts.

**Why it works**

Humans are wired for story. The three-act structure (situation → struggle → insight) creates tension and resolution, driving comments like 'I needed this today' - which are gold for the algorithm.

**🗂 Template: The Story Arc**

**LINE 1 - THE HOOK (one punchy sentence)**

_\[Surprising or counterintuitive outcome from your story\]_

**THE BUILD-UP (2-3 short paragraphs)**

_\[Context: set the scene, who you were / what you were doing\]_

_\[The problem or turning point: what went wrong or what you discovered\]_

_\[The struggle: what you tried, why it was hard\]_

**THE LESSON (1-2 sentences, bold takeaway)**

_\[The single insight or shift in thinking that came from it\]_

**CLOSING CTA**

Have you experienced this? Drop a comment below 👇

**EXAMPLE HOOK**

I got fired after my best quarter ever. Here's what I learned.

# **SECTION 2: Instagram**

**How the Algorithm Works**

Instagram uses separate ranking systems for the Feed, Reels, Stories, and Explore. The three most important signals confirmed by Instagram's head Adam Mosseri are: (1) watch time, (2) sends per reach - DM shares are 3-5× more valuable than likes, and (3) likes per reach. The algorithm initially tests content on a small slice of followers; strong early engagement unlocks Explore distribution. Original content receives 40-60% more distribution than reposts.

| **Metric**                   | **What to optimise for**                 |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Watch time / completion rate | Hook in first 3 seconds, no slow intros  |
| Sends per reach (DM shares)  | Create save-worthy or shareable content  |
| Saves                        | How-tos, tips, and educational carousels |
| Likes per reach              | Relatable, emotionally resonant content  |
| Best post times              | Tue-Fri, 11 AM-1 PM or 5-7 PM local time |

## **Pattern 1 - The Reels Hook + Payoff**

Reels are the fastest-growing surface on Instagram and the primary vehicle for reaching non-followers. The first 3 seconds must create a 'pattern interrupt' - something that stops the scroll. The payoff must arrive quickly. 10-20 seconds is the sweet spot for completion rate.

**Why it works**

Up to 50% of viewers drop off in the first 3 seconds. A 10-second Reel with 80% retention outperforms a 60-second Reel with 30% retention every time in terms of algorithmic distribution.

**🗂 Template: Reels Hook + Payoff**

**SECOND 0-3 - THE HOOK (on-screen text + visual/audio interrupt)**

_\[Contrarian statement, surprising question, or bold claim\]_

E.g. 'You've been doing \[X\] wrong' | 'POV: \[relatable situation\]'

**SECOND 3-15 - THE CONTENT**

_\[Deliver the value or punchline immediately - no padding\]_

_\[Use text overlays throughout - 50% of viewers watch on mute\]_

_\[Cut quickly - one new frame or idea every 2-3 seconds\]_

**LAST 2-3 SECONDS - THE CTA**

'Save this' | 'Share with someone who needs this' | 'Follow for more'

**CAPTION**

_\[One bold line - the hook restated\]_

_\[2-3 lines of context or extra value\]_

_\[CTA: 'Which tip was most useful? Comment below 👇'\]_

_\[3-5 hashtags: 3 broad + 2 niche\]_

**PROVEN REEL HOOK FORMULAS**

'Nobody talks about \[overlooked topic\]...'

'POV: \[relatable moment\]'

'3 signs you \[problem\] (and how to fix it)'

'Before vs. After: \[transformation\]'

## **Pattern 4 - The Before/After Transformation**

Before-and-after content creates a visual payoff that keeps viewers watching until the end - which directly improves completion rate and algorithmic reach. Works for any visual category: design, fitness, writing, code, products, spaces.

# **SECTION 3: Email**

**How the Inbox Works**

Email remains the highest-ROI marketing channel. In 2026, the metrics that matter most are Click-to-Open Rate (CTOR), which measures content quality among actual openers, and reply rate for cold email. Open rates are inflated by Apple's Mail Privacy Protection. Focus on CTOR (target: above 10%) and list health. The average B2B professional receives 120-150 emails per day - your email must earn its open.

| **Metric**                    | **2026 Benchmark / Target**                   |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Average open rate (all types) | 43% (inflated) - focus on CTOR instead        |
| Click-to-open rate (CTOR)     | Target >10%; avg across industries: 6.81%     |
| Cold email reply rate         | Avg 3.4%; aim for 8-15% with personalization  |
| Welcome email open rate       | 68.6% - highest of all email types            |
| Optimal email length (cold)   | 50-125 words achieves ~50% higher reply rates |
| Best send day (cold)          | Monday launch; Wednesday follow-up            |
| Best send time                | 9:30-11:30 AM recipient's local timezone      |

## **Pattern 1 - The Subject Line Formula (5 Types That Work)**

The subject line is the single biggest lever for open rate. Below are the five subject line patterns that consistently outperform, based on large-scale A/B testing data.

**🗂 Template: High-Performing Subject Lines**

**TYPE 1 - THE CURIOSITY GAP**

Formula: '\[Surprising result or claim\] (here's how)'

Example: 'We tripled our reply rate by deleting this one section'

**TYPE 2 - THE SPECIFIC NUMBER**

Formula: '\[Specific result\] in \[timeframe\]'

Example: '127 leads in 14 days - no ads'

**TYPE 3 - THE PROBLEM CALL-OUT**

Formula: '\[Pain point they recognise immediately\]?'

Example: 'Still losing deals at the proposal stage?'

**TYPE 4 - THE PERSONALISED OPENER**

Formula: '\[First name\], \[specific reference to them or their company\]'

Example: 'Sarah, saw your post about Q2 pipeline - quick thought'

**TYPE 5 - THE RE-ENGAGEMENT**

Formula: 'Did I lose you, \[first name\]?' | 'Last email (I promise)'

**RULES FOR ALL SUBJECT LINES**

Under 50 characters for mobile • No all-caps • No excessive punctuation

Avoid spam triggers: 'Free', 'Guaranteed', 'Act now', '% off'

A/B test every campaign - small changes move open rate by 5-15%

## **Pattern 2 - The Cold Email (Short-Form Outreach)**

The optimal cold email in 2026 is 50-125 words and laser-focused on a single, specific problem the recipient has. Generic pitches get deleted; personalised, problem-specific emails get replied to. Follow-up emails generate 42% of all replies - always send a sequence.

**🗂 Template: Cold Outreach Email**

**SUBJECT LINE**

_\[Use one of the 5 subject line types above - keep under 50 chars\]_

**LINE 1 - PERSONALISED OPENER (1 sentence)**

Reference something specific: their content, company news, a mutual connection, or a problem you know they have.

_\[E.g. 'Saw your post on \[topic\] - we solve exactly that problem for \[industry\] companies.'\]_

**LINE 2-3 - THE VALUE STATEMENT (1-2 sentences)**

State what you do and who you do it for.

_\[E.g. 'We help \[ICP\] achieve \[outcome\] without \[pain point\].'\]_

_\[Add one credibility signal: 'including \[recognisable client or result\]'\]_

**LINE 4 - THE ASK (1 sentence, soft and specific)**

Make the ask tiny - a quick reply, not a 30-minute call.

_\[E.g. 'Worth a 10-minute chat this week?'\]_

**SIGNATURE**

_\[First name\], \[Title\], \[Company\] | \[One-line value prop\]_

**FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE**

Day 1: Send original email

Day 3: Follow-up - add one new piece of value (case study, stat, tip)

Day 7: Final follow-up - 'Last email, I promise. Happy to connect whenever timing is right.'

## **Pattern 3 - The Newsletter / Value Email**

For warm lists (subscribers who opted in), the highest-performing format is the one-big-idea email: a single, focused insight, story, or framework delivered in 200-400 words. Readers open it because it reliably delivers value in under 3 minutes of reading.

**🗂 Template: The Value Newsletter**

**SUBJECT LINE**

_\[One-sentence tease of the insight, or a curiosity gap\]_

**OPENING LINE (hook - 1 sentence)**

_\[Start with a surprising statement, counter-intuitive fact, or short story opener\]_

**THE MAIN IDEA (150-250 words)**

_\[Paragraph 1: Set up the problem or context\]_

_\[Paragraph 2: Deliver the insight, framework, or story\]_

_\[Paragraph 3: Make it actionable - what should they do with this?\]_

**THE TAKEAWAY (1-2 sentences, bold)**

_\[Distil the whole email into one memorable sentence they can share\]_

**CTA (one, clear, specific)**

Choose ONE of:

• 'Reply and tell me \[relevant question\]'

• 'Click here to \[specific next step\]'

• 'Forward this to \[relevant person\]'

**P.S. LINE (optional but powerful)**

_\[Add a secondary CTA, a teaser for next week, or a piece of social proof\]_

**FORMAT RULES**

Short paragraphs (2-3 lines max) • One idea only - no topic-hopping

65-80 chars per line • Mobile-optimised • Consistent send day/time

## **Pattern 4 - The Automated Welcome Sequence**

Welcome emails achieve the highest open rates of any email type (68.6%). A well-structured 3-5 email welcome sequence sets expectations, delivers immediate value, and builds the relationship that makes future emails get opened. Automate this once and it compounds indefinitely.

**🗂 Template: Welcome Sequence (5 Emails)**

**EMAIL 1 - INSTANT (sent immediately on signup)**

Subject: 'Welcome - here's what to expect from me'

Content: Thank them, set expectations (what you send + how often), deliver the lead magnet if applicable, ask one question to segment them.

**EMAIL 2 - DAY 2**

Subject: '\[Your best piece of content / insight\]'

Content: Deliver your single best piece of free value - a framework, a guide, a story. Make them glad they signed up.

**EMAIL 3 - DAY 4**

Subject: 'The \[problem\] most \[audience\] face (and how to fix it)'

Content: Address the #1 pain point of your audience. Position yourself as the solution. Soft intro to your product/service.

**EMAIL 4 - DAY 7**

Subject: 'How \[customer name / type\] achieved \[result\]'

Content: A short case study or testimonial. Show the transformation. End with a clear CTA to a next step.

**EMAIL 5 - DAY 10**

Subject: 'One quick question for you, \[first name\]'

Content: Ask them what they're struggling with most. Use replies to improve future content AND as a segmentation signal.

**SEQUENCE PRINCIPLES**

Each email = one idea + one CTA • Always reply-worthy • Consistent sender name

# **SECTION 4: WhatsApp**

**How the Channel Works**

WhatsApp achieves 98% open rates and click rates up to 60% - far above email or any other marketing channel. In 2026 it is a full-fledged performance channel for sales, service, and marketing. All outbound marketing messages must use Meta-approved templates. The key distinction versus other channels: WhatsApp is a conversation, not a broadcast. Mass generic messages burn budget and get blocked. Short, personalised, behaviour-triggered messages convert. Limit proactive marketing messages to 1-2 per week per subscriber.

| **Metric**         | **What to optimise for**                                          |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Open rate          | 98% - message must earn its read with genuine value               |
| Click-through rate | Up to 60% - use clear, single CTAs with quick-reply buttons       |
| Message length     | Keep under 160 characters where possible; use emojis sparingly    |
| Send frequency     | 1-2 proactive messages per week max; triggered messages as needed |
| Best send time     | Tue-Thu, 10 AM-12 PM; avoid weekends for B2B                      |

## **Pattern 1 - The Flash Broadcast**

A short, time-limited offer or announcement sent to a segmented list. The combination of exclusivity ("WhatsApp subscribers only"), urgency (countdown), and personal delivery channel makes this the highest-converting WhatsApp format. One brand generated \$15,000 in revenue from a single broadcast to 10,000 contacts in under 72 hours.

**📋 Template: Flash Broadcast**

**LINE 1 - THE HOOK (personalised opener)**

_Hi \[First Name\] 👋 Quick heads up..._

**LINE 2 - THE OFFER (exclusive + specific)**

_For our WhatsApp subscribers only: \[offer/announcement in one sentence\]._

**LINE 3 - THE URGENCY**

_Only \[X\] spots / Ends \[time/date\]. First come, first served._

**LINE 4 - THE CTA (one button or one link)**

_Reply YES to claim yours → OR tap: \[link\]_

**EXAMPLE MESSAGE**

Hi Sarah 👋 Quick heads up - we just opened 10 spots for our new strategy session (WhatsApp subscribers only). 48-hour access, \$97. Tap to grab yours: \[link\]

## **Pattern 2 - The Conversational Nurture**

The most effective WhatsApp messages feel like a message from a friend, not a brand blast. This pattern sends a short value snippet - a tip, insight, or question - that invites a reply. Replies restart the 24-hour service window, improve deliverability, and build trust. The goal is to keep the user in the chat.

**📋 Template: Conversational Nurture**

**MESSAGE STRUCTURE**

_Hey \[First Name\] 👋_

_\[1-2 sentence value tip or insight - make it genuinely useful\]_

_\[Conversational question that invites a reply - easy to answer, one word or short phrase\]_

_Reply A or B - or just type your answer 👇_

**EXAMPLE**

Hey Sam 👋 Quick tip: most people lose sales at follow-up #2 because they wait too long. The sweet spot is 48 hours, not 7 days. Which follow-up method is working best for you right now? A) Email B) WhatsApp C) Call 👇

**WHY IT WORKS**

Replies signal to Meta that your messages are wanted, improving future deliverability. They also open a service window for deeper 1-to-1 conversation. Segment responders by answer to personalise the next message.

## **Pattern 3 - The Triggered Recovery Message**

Behaviour-triggered messages (cart abandonment, post-purchase, re-engagement after inactivity) are automated once and generate revenue on autopilot. WhatsApp cart recovery generates up to 6x more revenue per message than email. The trigger is the most important part - timing and context make the message feel helpful, not pushy.

**📋 Template: Triggered Recovery**

**CART ABANDONMENT (send 1-3 hours after abandonment)**

_Hey \[First Name\], you left something behind 🛒_

_Your \[product name\] is still in your cart. Want me to hold it for you?_

_➡ \[Complete purchase link\] | Reply STOP to unsubscribe_

**RE-ENGAGEMENT (send after 30-60 days of inactivity)**

_Hey \[First Name\] 👋 It's been a while. We've launched \[new thing\] since you were last here - thought you'd want to know. Interested? Reply YES and I'll share the details._

**KEY RULES FOR TRIGGERED MESSAGES**

Always include an opt-out option • Reference the specific behaviour that triggered the message • Send within the relevant time window • Single CTA only • Never send if the user already converted

WhatsApp Channels (launched 2023, growing fast in 2025-2026) allow one-way broadcast to subscribers who opt in. Unlike broadcast lists, Channels can reach unlimited followers without needing to be in their contacts. Use this for regular value content - tips, news, insights - on a predictable schedule. Think of it as a WhatsApp-native newsletter.

**📋 Template: WhatsApp Channel Newsletter**

**OPENING (emoji + category label)**

_💡 \[Category: TIP / INSIGHT / NEWS / TOOL OF THE WEEK\]_

**HEADLINE (bold, one line)**

_\*\[Bold headline - the key takeaway in one sentence\]\*_

_\[Explain or expand the headline in plain, direct language. No corporate speak. No padding.\]_

**THE ACTION (optional link or prompt)**

_🔗 Read more: \[link\] | OR 📌 Save this for later_

**CHANNEL BEST PRACTICES**

Post 3-5 times per week • Use a consistent format subscribers recognise • Mix text, images, and short voice notes • Repurpose your best LinkedIn and email content here • Promote your Channel in your other content to grow subscribers

# **Programmatic Content Production System**

Use these guidelines to turn the templates above into a repeatable, scalable content engine.

**Step 1 - Build Your Content Calendar**

Schedule one post per day using a platform rotation. A sample weekly rhythm:

| **Day**   | **Platform + Pattern**                                                               |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Monday    | LinkedIn - Contrarian Take or Numbered Framework + WhatsApp - Conversational Nurture |
| Tuesday   | Instagram - Reel (Hook + Payoff) + WhatsApp Channel - Newsletter post                |
| Wednesday | Email - Weekly value newsletter + WhatsApp - Flash Broadcast (if offer week)         |
| Thursday  | LinkedIn - Story Arc or Data Reveal + WhatsApp - Triggered Recovery (automated)      |
| Friday    | Instagram - Educational Carousel + WhatsApp Channel - Newsletter post                |
| Saturday  | LinkedIn - Carousel (Document Post)                                                  |
| Sunday    | Plan & batch next week's content                                                     |

**Step 2 - Batch Content Creation**

- Block 2-3 hours once per week to fill out templates for all posts
- Use the platform-specific template as your starting point every time
- Keep a swipe file of your best-performing posts to remix and reuse
- Repurpose: a LinkedIn carousel becomes an Instagram carousel becomes an email

**Step 3 - Hook Library (Build and Maintain)**

The hook is the highest-leverage part of any piece of content. Build a running list of proven hook formulas and fill them in for each new post:

**Universal Hook Formulas**

• '\[Number\] things nobody tells you about \[topic\]' • 'I \[did X\] for \[timeframe\]. Here's what happened.' • 'Stop \[common behaviour\]. Do this instead.' • 'The \[topic\] advice I wish I had \[timeframe\] ago.' • 'Everyone talks about \[common thing\]. Nobody talks about \[overlooked thing\].' • 'Unpopular opinion: \[contrarian belief in your niche\]' • 'This one \[action\] changed how I \[outcome\] forever.'

**Step 4 - Performance Loop**

- Review analytics weekly: note top 3 posts by reach, saves, and replies
- Identify the pattern - which template type drove the best result?
- Double down: create 2-3 more posts using that pattern next week
- Kill what doesn't work: if a pattern underperforms 3× in a row, retire it
- Test one new pattern per month to keep the system fresh

**Remember: The Best Content Is Specific, Not Generic**

Templates are a starting point. Fill them with your genuine story, real data, and original perspective. The algorithm rewards engagement - and people engage with content that feels human.
