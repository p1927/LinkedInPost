# Telegram Formatting Rules

## Character / Length Limits
- **Message**: 4,096 characters maximum per message
- **Caption (media)**: 1,024 characters
- **Channel post**: 4,096 characters (can be extended with formatting)

## Formatting Syntax (MarkdownV2 / HTML)
Telegram supports two formatting modes: MarkdownV2 and HTML. For plain text output, apply formatting as follows:

- **Bold**: `**bold**` (MarkdownV2) or `<b>bold</b>` (HTML)
- **Italic**: `_italic_` (MarkdownV2) or `<i>italic</i>` (HTML)
- **Underline**: `__underline__` (MarkdownV2) or `<u>underline</u>` (HTML)
- **Strikethrough**: `~~strikethrough~~` (MarkdownV2) or `<s>strikethrough</s>` (HTML)
- **Monospace/code**: `` `code` `` (MarkdownV2) or `<code>code</code>` (HTML)
- **Code block**: ` ```language\ncode\n``` ` (MarkdownV2) or `<pre>code</pre>` (HTML)
- **Hyperlinks**: `[text](url)` (MarkdownV2) or `<a href="url">text</a>` (HTML)

## Hashtag Rules
- **Hashtags are functional in Telegram** — they create clickable topic links within the same channel or group
- Recommended: 1–3 hashtags per post
- Place hashtags at the end of the message
- Telegram searches within the channel use hashtags for navigation

## Content Rules
- Telegram channels support longer content than WhatsApp — up to 4,096 characters per post
- Posts in public channels are indexed by Telegram search and Google
- Use line breaks and headers (bold text as headings) to structure longer posts
- Emojis are supported and commonly used for visual structure
- External links work normally and are trusted by Telegram users
- Native polls, quizzes, and file attachments extend engagement options

## Channel vs Group
- **Channels**: Broadcast only; admins post, members read and react; suited for editorial content
- **Groups**: Two-way conversation; posts should invite replies and discussion
- Adjust CTA language accordingly ("Join the discussion →" for groups vs "Forward to a friend" for channels)
