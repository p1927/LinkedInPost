# Evergreen Trends Reference

## Recurring Business Trends

### Calendar-Driven Cycles
- **Q1 Planning Season** (January–February): Goal-setting, OKRs, budget allocations, fresh-start narratives. Audiences receptive to frameworks, checklists, and strategic planning content.
- **Performance Review Cycles** (typically Q2 and Q4): Self-advocacy, documentation of wins, negotiation prep, promotion conversations. High anxiety + high intent to act.
- **Conference Season** (March–May, September–October): Industry gatherings, keynote takeaways, networking discourse. Good for hot-take reactions and synthesis content.
- **Hiring Seasons** (January–March and September–November): Job market activity spikes. Career pivots, resume tips, interviewing skills, salary negotiation content resonates heavily.
- **Budget Planning** (October–December): CFO mindset, ROI framing, cost-cutting vs. investment debates. B2B audiences particularly attuned.
- **Summer Slowdown** (July–August): Decision-making slows; reflective, relationship-building, and educational content outperforms promotional content.
- **Back-to-School / Back-to-Work** (late August–September): Reset energy, new routines, productivity resets. "Fresh start" framing works well.
- **End-of-Year Reflections** (November–December): Lessons learned, year-in-review, gratitude, predictions for the coming year.
- **New Year Goals** (December–January): Aspirational, transformational framing. High engagement with habits, resolutions, and bold predictions.
- **Holiday Marketing** (November–December): Gift guides, urgency, limited time, generosity themes. B2C skews heavily; B2B uses "give the gift of" metaphor.

## GenZ / Internet Vocabulary Reference

Use these terms only when the audience skews younger (under ~35) or the tone is deliberately casual/playful. If there is any chance the audience may not recognize a term, either avoid it or briefly explain it parenthetically.

| Term | Meaning | Usage Note |
|------|---------|------------|
| no cap | "no lie," "for real" | Casual sincerity; works in punchy list items |
| slay | To execute something excellently | Celebratory; works for wins and achievements |
| bet | Agreement / affirmation / "got it" | Short, punchy; good for CTAs or conversational tone |
| lowkey / highkey | Subtly / very much so | Softens or amplifies a claim naturally |
| main character energy | Acting as if you're the protagonist of your own story | Good for personal branding and career content |
| understood the assignment | Delivered exactly what was needed | Complimenting execution; works for case studies |
| rent free | Something living in your head without invitation | Good for recurring themes or obsessive ideas |
| era (as in "I'm in my X era") | A defined chapter of life or focus | Career pivots, personal rebranding |
| delulu | Delusional in a self-aware, aspirational way | Ambition framing; risky—can feel forced |
| vibe check | Quick assessment of mood or alignment | Team dynamics, culture fit topics |
| touch grass | Go outside / disconnect from digital life | Digital wellness, burnout content |
| rizz | Natural charm or magnetism | Personal brand, charisma, communication skills |
| based | Holding an opinion confidently regardless of what others think | Contrarian takes; thought leadership |
| mid | Mediocre, nothing special | Critical takes; use carefully to avoid alienating |
| bussin | Exceptionally good | Enthusiastic endorsements |
| it's giving | Giving off a certain vibe or energy | Describing tone, aesthetics, brand identity |

## Cultural Touchpoints by Domain

### Tech
- **AI Hype Cycle**: Model releases, AI replacing jobs, "prompt engineering" discourse, AGI speculation, AI ethics debates
- **Layoff Discourse**: Big Tech workforce reductions, survivor guilt, talent market shifts, job security anxiety
- **Remote Work Debate**: RTO mandates vs. distributed teams, hybrid models, productivity studies, office culture
- **Startup Culture**: Hustle culture critiques, funding environment, founder mental health, "move fast" vs. sustainability

### Business
- **Quiet Quitting**: Doing the minimum required; boundary-setting at work; engagement debates
- **Return to Office (RTO)**: Employer mandates, employee resistance, commercial real estate implications
- **Creator Economy**: Monetization of expertise, content as career, platform dependency risks
- **Personal Branding**: LinkedIn optimization, thought leadership, "building in public"

### Career
- **Career Cushioning**: Keeping options open while still employed; passive job searching
- **Rage Applying**: Mass-applying to jobs out of frustration with current role
- **Bare Minimum Monday**: Easing into the work week intentionally; hustle culture backlash
- **Soft Life / Anti-Hustle**: Prioritizing rest, ease, and joy over grinding

## Usage Rules

1. **Audience gating**: GenZ slang only when target audience is under ~35 or tone is explicitly casual/playful. Never in formal B2B or executive-level content.
2. **Explain if uncertain**: If there is a meaningful chance the audience may not know the term, explain it in parentheses or avoid it altogether.
3. **Authenticity over trend-chasing**: A trend that feels forced is worse than no trend at all. Use sparingly and only when it genuinely fits.
4. **Recency awareness**: Evergreen cycles are always relevant; internet slang has a short shelf life. Validate slang currency before use.
5. **Cultural sensitivity**: Avoid trends rooted in tragedy, controversy, or political division. Never exploit a crisis for engagement.
