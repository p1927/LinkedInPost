# Email Formatting Rules

## Character / Length Limits
- **Subject line**: 40–60 characters optimal for mobile (max 78 before truncation)
- **Preview text**: 85–140 characters visible in inbox before opening
- **Body length**: No hard limit; 200–500 words performs best for newsletters; transactional emails should be shorter
- **Line length**: 60–80 characters per line for readability

## Formatting Rules
- **HTML vs plain text**: Plain text or minimal HTML often achieves better deliverability and feels more personal
- **Single-column layout**: Safest for mobile rendering (>60% of opens on mobile)
- **Font size**: 14–16px body text; 18–22px for headings
- **Line spacing**: 1.4–1.6x line height improves readability
- **Bold**: Use sparingly to highlight key phrases; do not bold entire paragraphs
- **Links**: Descriptive anchor text ("Read the guide") outperforms bare URLs
- **CTA button**: One primary CTA per email; place above the fold and repeat at the bottom for long emails

## Hashtag Rules
- **Hashtags do not apply in email** — omit all hashtags from email content
- Social platform tags (#hashtag, @handle) are irrelevant and look out of place in email

## Subject Line Rules
- Avoid all-caps words and excessive punctuation (spam filter triggers)
- Personalization tokens (first name) improve open rates when used contextually, not generically
- Curiosity and specificity outperform vague teasers
- Numbers in subject lines signal concrete value ("3 ways to…")

## Deliverability Notes
- Avoid spam trigger words: "free", "guaranteed", "act now", "limited time offer"
- Authenticate with SPF, DKIM, DMARC
- Maintain list hygiene — remove inactive subscribers to preserve sender reputation
- Plain text version should accompany every HTML email
