# Instagram Formatting Rules

## Character Limits
- **Caption**: 2,200 characters maximum
- **Visible before "more"**: ~125 characters (approximately 2 lines on mobile)
- **Hashtag recommendation**: 5–15 hashtags per post
- **Bio**: 150 characters

## "More" Fold Behavior
- Only ~125 characters are visible in the feed before the reader taps "more"
- The opening sentence must function as a standalone hook
- Avoid starting with hashtags or @mentions — lead with the compelling statement

## Formatting Rules
- **Line breaks**: Instagram does not render markdown; use blank lines for paragraph separation
- **Emojis**: Standard and encouraged — used as bullet substitutes and visual breaks
- **Lists**: Use emojis or dashes as list markers; no markdown rendering
- **Hashtags**: Can be placed inline or at the end; end-placement is cleaner for readability
- **Calls to action**: Place CTA before hashtag block, not after

## Hashtag Strategy
- Mix: niche tags (under 500K posts), mid-tier (500K–2M), broad (2M+)
- 5–15 hashtags is the current effective range
- Hashtag block can be separated from caption by line breaks for visual cleanliness
- Avoid banned or overused hashtags

## Platform Notes
- Caption text supports emojis natively
- No clickable links in captions — direct users to link in bio
- Carousel posts (multiple images) receive higher saves and shares
- Reels captions follow the same rules
