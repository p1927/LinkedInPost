# Palette Strategies

## Overview

A palette strategy defines the relationship between colors used in a composition. The choice of strategy communicates a distinct visual intention — from harmonious calm to dynamic tension.

---

## Strategies

### Complementary
Colors positioned opposite each other on the color wheel. Creates maximum contrast and visual tension, drawing attention to the focal point.

- **Characteristic**: High energy, bold differentiation
- **Typical Pairings**: Red/Green, Blue/Orange, Yellow/Purple
- **Best For**: Urgent content, calls-to-action, content that needs to stop the scroll
- **Intensity Match**: High intensity emotions (7–10)

### Analogous
Colors adjacent on the color wheel. Creates a harmonious, unified feeling with subtle variation.

- **Characteristic**: Cohesive, flowing, emotionally consistent
- **Typical Pairings**: Blue/Purple/Indigo, Yellow/Orange/Red, Green/Teal/Blue
- **Best For**: Storytelling content, reflective posts, brand-building narratives
- **Intensity Match**: Medium intensity emotions (4–7)

### Monochromatic
Multiple shades, tints, and tones of a single hue. Communicates depth and sophistication without distraction.

- **Characteristic**: Refined, focused, authoritative
- **Typical Pairings**: Dark navy + medium blue + light sky, Deep forest green + sage + mint
- **Best For**: Professional insights, authoritative thought leadership, subtle emotional resonance
- **Intensity Match**: Low to medium intensity emotions (1–5)

---

## Contrast Levels

### High Contrast
Strong differentiation between foreground and background. Text is unambiguous, visual hierarchy is sharp.

- **When to Use**: Mobile-first content, loud emotional tone, urgent messaging
- **Accessibility Note**: Meets WCAG AA for body text at minimum
- **Intensity Range**: 7–10

### Medium Contrast
Balanced differentiation. Clear hierarchy without visual aggression.

- **When to Use**: Standard professional content, mixed audience contexts, narrative posts
- **Accessibility Note**: Acceptable for headline text; supplement body with darker tones
- **Intensity Range**: 4–7

### Subtle Contrast
Low-key differentiation. Elegant, understated visual communication.

- **When to Use**: Thought leadership, introspective content, audiences that favor refinement over volume
- **Accessibility Note**: Requires careful pairing; avoid for body text without sufficient luminance difference
- **Intensity Range**: 1–4

## Strategy Selection Guide

| Intensity | Palette Strategy | Contrast Level |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| 8–10      | Complementary   | High           |
| 5–7       | Analogous       | Medium         |
| 1–4       | Monochromatic   | Subtle         |
