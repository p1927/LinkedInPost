# Power Words

Power words are high-signal vocabulary choices that reliably trigger specific psychological responses. Used deliberately, they amplify the emotional and persuasive effect of surrounding copy. Used carelessly, they read as manipulative or generic.

---

## Urgency

Words that create a sense of time pressure or consequence for delay.

**Words**: now, today, immediately, finally, before it's too late, last chance, critical, urgent, deadline, limited window, closing, expiring, this week, this quarter, right now, no more waiting

**Usage rule**: Only deploy urgency language when a genuine time constraint exists in the content's context. Manufactured urgency erodes trust with sophisticated audiences. Anchor urgency to real conditions (market shifts, deadlines, irreversible trends).

---

## Curiosity

Words and phrases that open a knowledge gap the reader must close.

**Words**: secret, what nobody tells you, hidden, rarely discussed, overlooked, surprising, counterintuitive, unexpected, the real reason, what most people miss, what actually happens, the truth about, behind the scenes, beneath the surface, few people know

**Usage rule**: Curiosity words work best in hooks and section openers. The content must deliver on the implied promise — failing to close the curiosity gap is a trust-destroying pattern.

---

## Authority

Words that signal expertise, credentials, and informed perspective.

**Words**: research shows, data confirms, proven, evidence-based, tested, studied, after X years, across X companies, the pattern we see, consistently, systematically, professionally, established, expert, credentialed, peer-reviewed, industry-wide

**Usage rule**: Pair authority words with specific claims. "Research shows" with no citation is weaker than "a 2023 McKinsey study of 400 companies found." Authority language must be earned by what follows it.

---

## Exclusivity

Words that signal access to something not everyone gets.

**Words**: exclusive, insider, only, select, limited, privileged, behind the scenes, inner circle, by invitation, rare, not public, early access, private, off-the-record, between us, few people have

**Usage rule**: Exclusivity language activates scarcity and social proof simultaneously. Use sparingly — overuse makes content feel like clickbait. Best deployed once per post as a deliberate signal.

---

## Emotion

Words that activate feeling rather than logic.

**Words**: heartbreaking, transformative, devastating, thrilling, terrifying, profound, gut-wrenching, exhilarating, crushing, liberating, shocking, moving, raw, honest, vulnerable, fearless, relentless, passionate, driven

**Usage rule**: Match emotional intensity to content context. High-intensity emotion words in a low-stakes post feel tone-deaf. Reserve emotionally heavy words for moments in the post that genuinely warrant them.

---

## Simplicity

Words that reduce perceived effort and cognitive load.

**Words**: simple, easy, clear, fast, quick, step-by-step, effortless, straightforward, no jargon, in plain terms, no complexity, just, only, one thing, single, three steps, two minutes

**Usage rule**: Simplicity words are promises. If the content that follows is actually complicated, these words backfire. Use them only when the underlying advice or process is genuinely accessible.

---

## Results

Words that anchor the reader to outcomes and tangible value.

**Words**: results, outcome, impact, achieve, gain, grow, build, double, triple, increase, save, earn, generate, reduce, eliminate, transform, unlock, accelerate, scale, win

**Usage rule**: Results words are most powerful when paired with specific metrics or time frames. "Grow your audience" is weaker than "grow your audience by 40% in 90 days." Quantify wherever credible specificity is available.

---

## Community

Words that signal belonging, shared identity, and collective experience.

**Words**: we, us, together, our, team, community, fellow, peers, colleagues, everyone building, those of us who, the people in this room, you already know this, you've been there, most of us, we've all

**Usage rule**: Community words activate the liking and social proof principles. Especially effective in posts addressing shared frustrations or celebrating shared wins. Avoid if the community reference doesn't ring authentic for the audience.

---

## General Usage Rules

1. **One category per section**: Mixing too many power word categories in a single sentence dilutes all of them.
2. **Placement matters**: Power words hit hardest in the hook, at the start of key insight sentences, and in the CTA.
3. **Specificity beats volume**: Five precisely placed power words outperform thirty scattered ones.
4. **Match to audience register**: Junior audiences respond to simplicity and results words. Senior audiences respond to authority and exclusivity words.
5. **Never use power words to make weak claims feel stronger**: They amplify the signal — they do not substitute for substance.
