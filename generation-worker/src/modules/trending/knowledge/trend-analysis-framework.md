# Trend Analysis Framework

A structured approach for evaluating whether a trend is worth incorporating into content and how to do it well.

---

## 1. Relevance Scoring (1–10)

How closely does this trend relate to the topic being written about?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 9–10 | The trend IS the topic, or directly shapes the audience's current reality |
| 7–8 | Strong thematic overlap; natural integration point |
| 5–6 | Adjacent connection; works as a reference or metaphor |
| 3–4 | Loose association; only works with creative bridging |
| 1–2 | Trend-jacking; forced connection that dilutes credibility |

**Decision rule**: Score ≥ 6 to reference the trend at all. Score ≥ 8 to make it a primary angle.

---

## 2. Freshness Scoring (1–10)

How current is this trend right now?

| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 10 | Breaking this week; first-mover advantage |
| 8–9 | This week to this month; still very timely |
| 5–7 | This month to this quarter; relevant but not hot |
| 3–4 | Evergreen / cyclical (e.g., Q1 planning season) |
| 1–2 | Fading or stale; may signal you're behind the curve |

**Note**: Lower freshness is not always bad. Evergreen (score 3–4) is often the safest bet for content with a longer shelf life.

---

## 3. Audience Fit

Would this persona actually engage with this trend? Evaluate against:

- **Concerns**: Does the trend speak to what keeps them up at night?
- **Habits**: Is this something they already talk about or consume?
- **Language register**: Does the trend's vocabulary match how this persona communicates?
- **Platform behavior**: Is this trend circulating where this persona spends time (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Reddit, newsletters)?
- **Professional identity**: Would referencing this trend make the persona feel seen, or alienated?

**Green lights**: Trend is already in the persona's feed; they have an opinion on it; it connects to their stated concerns or ambitions.

**Red flags**: Trend is from a different demographic; persona's community hasn't adopted the language; the trend may feel condescending or try-hard.

---

## 4. Cultural Sensitivity Check

Before using any trend, confirm it passes these gates:

- **Origin check**: Does this trend have roots in tragedy, exploitation, or marginalization? If so, avoid unless the purpose is to honor that origin respectfully.
- **Political neutrality**: Does referencing this trend force the content to take a political side? If yes, avoid — content that triggers political responses alienates half the audience.
- **Controversy proximity**: Is this trend currently entangled in ongoing controversy or legal/ethical debate? Wait until dust settles or avoid entirely.
- **Punch-down test**: Does using this trend mock, diminish, or exclude a group of people? Never use trends that punch down.

---

## 5. Integration Techniques

### Natural Reference (Low intensity)
Weave the trend into one line or phrase without drawing attention to the reference. The audience who knows it gets a nod; those who don't aren't confused.
> Example: "Q4 is approaching and, no cap, most teams aren't ready for the planning cycle they're about to face."

### Speaking the Language (Medium intensity)
Use the trend's vocabulary or framing as the lens through which the topic is discussed. Makes the audience feel like the content was written for them specifically.
> Example: "If you're in your 'career cushioning era,' here's what to prioritize before you start applying..."

### Making it the Focus (High intensity)
The trend is the headline. The content exists because of the trend. Only do this when relevance score is ≥ 8 and freshness is high.
> Example: "Everyone's rage applying right now. Here's why most of those applications won't convert — and what to do instead."

### Trend-Jacking (Avoid)
Forcing a connection between an unrelated trend and your topic for the sake of attention. Destroys credibility. Detectable and cringe-worthy. Do not do this.

---

## 6. Confidence Calibration

Rate confidence that this trend is genuinely current and relevant:

| Level | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| High (8–10) | Multiple independent sources confirm; trending on major platforms; recent news coverage |
| Medium (5–7) | Single strong source; trending in a niche community; anecdotal but consistent |
| Low (1–4) | Single source; may be regional or niche; unverified; no corroboration |

**When confidence is low**: Either skip the trend or hedge with language like "emerging signal" or "worth watching." Never present low-confidence trends as established facts.

---

## 7. Timeliness Window

How long before this trend feels stale in the content?

| Trend Type | Typical Window |
|-----------|---------------|
| Breaking news / viral moment | 24–72 hours |
| Weekly discourse (LinkedIn/Twitter) | 1–2 weeks |
| Monthly narrative (industry shift) | 1–3 months |
| Quarterly cycle (Q-planning, review season) | Duration of the quarter |
| Annual hook (year-end reflections, New Year) | 4–6 weeks around the event |
| Evergreen cultural reference | 6–18 months (monitor for decay) |
| GenZ slang | Highly variable; assume 3–12 months before saturation |

**Rule of thumb**: If you cannot confidently place this trend within its timeliness window, treat it as evergreen or skip it.

---

## Summary Decision Matrix

Before using a trend in content, score it:

| Dimension | Minimum to Proceed |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Relevance | ≥ 6 |
| Freshness | ≥ 3 (evergreen acceptable) |
| Audience Fit | Green light on at least 2 of 5 checks |
| Cultural Sensitivity | Pass all 4 gates |
| Confidence | ≥ 5 (medium or high) |

If any dimension fails its minimum, either replace the trend or downgrade its role (e.g., drop from headline to single-line reference).
