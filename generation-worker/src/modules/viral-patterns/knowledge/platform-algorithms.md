# Platform Algorithm Signals

Each platform ranks and distributes content based on a distinct set of signals. Optimizing for the right signals on each platform increases organic reach.

---

## LinkedIn

**Primary distribution signal:** Dwell time (how long a viewer pauses on the post in the feed).

**Key signals (ranked by impact):**
1. **Early engagement velocity** — Reactions and comments in the first 60–90 minutes trigger wider distribution. Prioritize hooks that convert skimmers into engagers.
2. **Comment depth** — Multi-turn comment threads signal high-value content. Questions and provocative statements drive replies.
3. **Dwell time** — Long posts with clear paragraph breaks and white space slow scrolling. The "see more" click counts as engagement.
4. **Native content preference** — External links in body copy suppress reach. Place URLs in the first comment if needed.
5. **Connection proximity** — First- and second-degree engagement amplifies distribution. Tagging relevant people (sparingly) accelerates seeding.
6. **Hashtag relevance** — 3–5 targeted hashtags connect the post to topic feeds. Niche hashtags outperform oversaturated ones.
7. **Consistency signals** — Regular posting cadence establishes creator status and receives algorithmic favor.

**Content format winners:** Text-only posts, carousels (PDF documents), short-form video. Images underperform text.

---

## Instagram

**Primary distribution signal:** Saves and shares (treated as higher-intent than likes or comments).

**Key signals (ranked by impact):**
1. **Saves** — The single strongest signal. Content that is "worth keeping" (tutorials, templates, frameworks, lists) maximizes saves.
2. **Shares to Stories / DMs** — Direct shares indicate high personal relevance.
3. **Watch time (Reels)** — Completion rate and replays are weighted heavily; front-load the most compelling content.
4. **Comments** — Substantive comments (more than emoji-only) boost ranking. Questions that invite text responses perform best.
5. **Hashtag strategy** — Mix of niche (under 500K posts), mid-tier (500K–2M), and broad tags. 5–15 hashtags is the current sweet spot.
6. **Carousel swipe-through rate** — Each swipe signals interest; use cliffhangers between slides.
7. **Posting time** — Publish when your audience is active; first-hour engagement shapes initial distribution.

**Content format winners:** Carousels, Reels, infographic-style single images. Plain text performs poorly without a visual hook.

## Email

**Primary distribution signal:** Open rate and click-through rate (CTR).

**Key signals (ranked by impact):**
1. **Subject line** — The single most important element. Curiosity, specificity, and personalization drive opens. 40–60 characters is optimal for mobile.
2. **Sender name recognition** — Familiar sender names outperform generic brand names. Build personal brand in the "from" field.
3. **Preview text** — The 85–140 characters visible before opening complement the subject line; treat it as a second headline.
4. **Mobile rendering** — Over 60% of emails are opened on mobile. Single-column layouts, 14–16px font, and short paragraphs reduce abandonment.
5. **Engagement history** — ISPs weight recent engagement signals. Re-engagement campaigns and list hygiene protect deliverability.
6. **Link placement** — Above-the-fold CTA maximizes CTR; repetition at the end of long emails captures late converters.
7. **Plain text vs. HTML** — Plain-text or minimal-HTML emails often achieve higher deliverability and feel more personal.

**Content format winners:** Short, conversational narratives. Bullet-heavy emails lose readers. One primary CTA per email.

## WhatsApp / Telegram

**Primary distribution signal:** Forward rate (organic peer-to-peer sharing).

**Key signals (ranked by impact):**
1. **Immediate utility** — Content that solves a problem right now gets forwarded immediately. Practical, actionable formats win.
2. **Conciseness** — Walls of text are rarely forwarded. Short messages with clear formatting (line breaks, bold via *asterisks* or **markdown**) maximize reading.
3. **Emotional resonance** — Humor, inspiration, and moral outrage are the three dominant sharing emotions in private messaging contexts.
4. **Trust signal** — Content from credible or known sources is forwarded more. Author attribution or a recognizable publication name helps.
5. **Low friction to share** — All content fits in a single forward action with no login, paywall, or redirect. Self-contained content outperforms link-dependent content.
6. **Group fit** — Content tailored to a specific community identity (industry group, alumni network, interest group) spreads faster within targeted communities.
7. **Multimedia hooks** — Images, short videos, and voice notes receive higher engagement than text alone in messaging apps; visuals trigger immediate reactions.

**Content format winners:** Short punchy text with line breaks, voice notes, short video clips, memes, infographics. Long articles rarely get read or forwarded.
