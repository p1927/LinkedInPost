# Persuasion Frameworks

## AIDA

**Description**: Classic awareness-to-action funnel. Moves an audience through four sequential psychological states — from noticing the content to taking deliberate action.

**Structure**:
1. Attention — open with a hook that disrupts the scroll or breaks a mental pattern
2. Interest — build relevance by connecting to the reader's specific context or problem
3. Desire — shift from "this is interesting" to "I want this" by showing the outcome
4. Action — give a clear, low-friction next step

**Best for**: Promotional content, product launches, event announcements, opportunity framing, job postings. Works best with audiences already somewhat familiar with the topic.

---

## PAS

**Description**: Problem-Agitate-Solve. Leads with a pain point the reader already feels, intensifies it to create urgency, then presents a resolution. Emotionally direct and high-conversion.

**Structure**:
1. Problem — name a specific, recognizable frustration or failure state
2. Agitate — show why it is worse than it seems, what it costs long-term, or what it blocks
3. Solve — introduce the fix, reframe, or insight that resolves the tension

**Best for**: Pain-aware audiences, B2B content, thought leadership addressing known industry dysfunctions, advisory posts, turnaround stories.

---

## BAB

**Description**: Before-After-Bridge. Contrasts the current undesirable state with an aspirational future state, then explains how to get from one to the other. Optimistic and forward-looking.

**Structure**:
1. Before — paint the current situation (struggles, constraints, mediocrity)
2. After — describe the transformed state (specific, vivid, emotionally resonant)
3. Bridge — reveal the mechanism, insight, or action that enables the shift

**Best for**: Transformation stories, career/life pivots, product positioning, motivational content, audience in contemplation stage.

---

## 4Ps

**Description**: Promise-Picture-Proof-Push. Leads with a bold claim, makes it tangible with a vivid scenario, grounds it in evidence, then drives action. Balances aspiration with credibility.

**Structure**:
1. Promise — state a specific, meaningful outcome the reader can achieve
2. Picture — make it concrete and sensory ("imagine your Monday morning when...")
3. Proof — validate with data, case studies, credentials, or social proof
4. Push — give a directive call to action with urgency or a reason to act now

**Best for**: Authority-building content, high-claim thought leadership, educational posts with a strong POV, monetization-adjacent posts.

---

## Monroe's Motivated Sequence

**Description**: Five-step sequence developed for speeches; adapts well to long-form LinkedIn posts. Moves the audience through need-recognition to committed action in a psychologically logical arc.

**Structure**:
1. Attention — capture focus immediately with a surprising fact, question, or tension
2. Need — establish the gap between current state and desired state; make it personal
3. Satisfaction — present the solution, framework, or recommendation in full
4. Visualization — help the audience experience the post-solution reality concretely
5. Action — close with a specific, achievable next step the reader can take today

**Best for**: Long-form posts (1000+ characters), opinion pieces, calls to change behavior, complex topics requiring reader buy-in, posts targeting cold or skeptical audiences.

## Framework Selection Rules

### By Content Length

| Length | Recommended Frameworks |
|--------|------------------------|
| Short (< 400 chars) | PAS, BAB |
| Medium (400–900 chars) | AIDA, PAS, BAB, 4Ps |
| Long (900+ chars) | Monroe's Motivated Sequence, 4Ps, AIDA |

### By Content Type

| Content Type | Best Framework |
|--------------|----------------|
| Pain point / rant | PAS |
| Transformation story | BAB |
| Product or opportunity | AIDA |
| Credibility / authority | 4Ps |
| Industry call-to-action | Monroe's Motivated Sequence |
| How-to / educational | AIDA or 4Ps |
| Contrarian take | PAS or BAB |
| Data-led insight | 4Ps |

### By Audience Awareness Level

- **Unaware of problem**: BAB (show them the gap first)
- **Problem-aware**: PAS (agitate what they already feel)
- **Solution-aware**: AIDA or 4Ps (channel toward action)
- **Skeptical / critical**: Monroe's Motivated Sequence (earn trust step by step)
